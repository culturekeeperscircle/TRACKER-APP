# THE CANONICAL RESEARCH QUESTION

> Which laws and policies from [GOVERNMENT ENTITY] will severely harm the cultural resources (People, Places, Practices, and Treasures) paramount to the cultural continuity of [CULTURAL COMMUNITY], moderately harm those same cultural resources, or protect those same cultural resources?

Where:

- **[GOVERNMENT ENTITY]** is the federal entity in the item (White House, executive agency, Congress, federal court, international body).
- **[CULTURAL COMMUNITY]** is one of the five primary ethnic communities the tracker covers:
  1. **Indigenous** (includes Native American nations, Alaska Native, Native Hawaiian)
  2. **African-descendant** (includes African American, Afro-Caribbean, Afro-Latiné, African diaspora)
  3. **Latiné** (Latin American, Chicano, Hispanic, Latinx)
  4. **Asian** (Asian American, AANHPI, South Asian, Southeast Asian, East Asian)
  5. **Pacific Islander** (Native Hawaiian, Samoan, Tongan, Chamorro, Polynesian, Micronesian, Melanesian)

Items may also touch secondary communities (LGBTQ+, immigrants, women, disabled, faith communities, etc.). Code those as additional affected communities. The PRIMARY focus of the tracker is the cultural continuity of the five ethnic communities above.

# WHAT "CULTURAL CONTINUITY" MEANS

A law or policy affects cultural continuity when it shapes a community's capacity to persist across generations as a distinct cultural entity. Continuity operates along four resource dimensions (the PPPT frame):

- **People** — demographic survival, civil rights, dignified existence, community leadership, migration and assembly rights, health and education access, immigration status, safety from hate violence.
- **Places** — sacred sites, heritage sites, tribal lands, historic districts, cultural neighborhoods, diaspora homelands, museums, archives, cultural centers.
- **Practices** — religious and spiritual observance, language transmission, foodways, folk arts, ceremonies, dance and music traditions, oral histories, traditional ecological knowledge.
- **Treasures** — material culture, artifacts, archives, intellectual property, historic documents, ancestral remains, sacred objects, artistic works, inherited land and assets.

# THREE VALID THREAT LEVELS

- **SEVERE** — Direct, immediate, often irreversible harm to one or more PPPT resources paramount to the cultural continuity of at least one primary ethnic community.
- **HARMFUL** — Significant but potentially reversible harm to those resources.
- **PROTECTIVE** — Safeguards, restores, funds, or defends those resources.

Use **WATCH** only when the item signals a developing threat that has not yet crystallized.

# WHAT COUNTS AS "PARAMOUNT" FOR EACH COMMUNITY

- **Indigenous.** NAGPRA, sacred-site protection, tribal consultation, treaty rights, land-into-trust, Indian education, tribal sovereignty, Alaska Native subsistence, Native Hawaiian self-determination, ICWA.
- **African-descendant.** HBCU funding, National Museum of African American History and Culture, historically Black neighborhoods, reparations, Juneteenth, voting rights, criminal-justice policy disproportionately affecting Black communities, Gullah/Geechee preservation, Reconstruction Era National Monument, Freedmen descendants.
- **Latiné.** Immigration protection (DACA, TPS, asylum), language access and bilingual education, farmworker protections, border policy for mixed-status families, Spanish-language cultural institutions, Latin American heritage sites, food and land sovereignty.
- **Asian.** AAPI heritage recognition, hate-crime protections, disaggregated data, immigration and visa policy, language access for Asian languages, cultural institutions (Smithsonian Asian Pacific American Museum, Japanese American incarceration sites), faith communities (Sikh, Hindu, Buddhist, Muslim when Asian-diaspora).
- **Pacific Islander.** Native Hawaiian homestead and land rights, Compact of Free Association (Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Palau) and the Pacific Islander diaspora in the U.S., sacred sites, voyaging traditions, climate-displacement policy, military-base impact on Pacific communities.

# ALWAYS MARK AS RELEVANT (High Confidence)

Any item involving:

- English-only or official-language legislation → HARMFUL (Latiné, Asian, Pacific Islander cultural continuity)
- Language access, interpreter rights, translation services
- Reproductive rights legislation (all five communities via maternal health and birth equity)
- Immigration reform, DACA, TPS, asylum, deportation, visa policy (Latiné, Asian, Pacific Islander primary)
- Cultural institution legislation (Smithsonian museums, Kennedy Center, NEA, NEH, IMLS, CPB, HBCU funding, tribal colleges)
- NAGPRA, NHPA Section 106, sacred-site protection, tribal consultation (Indigenous)
- Voting rights and election access (all five communities)
- DEI rescissions or expansions (all five communities)
- Hate-crime legislation (all five communities)
- Any bill or resolution naming a specific ethnic, racial, or cultural community

# SCOPE FILTER: FEDERAL ACTIONS ONLY
